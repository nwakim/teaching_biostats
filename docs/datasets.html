Teaching Dataset Repository

Using these datasets in your classroom

If you are speaking about social issues and you have not experienced the consequences/oppression/bias associated with the issue, please take time to educate yourself on the experiences of folks who have. Within each dataset page, I have included some references to get you started on this.

This repository is not meant to be the only resource or attempt to do the work of other, existing resources. There are many wonderful, socially inclined resources (like ICSPR) and statistically inclined resources, and teaching inclined resources. I hope to connect these three areas to make a repository that helps connect professors teaching statistics with statistical lessons and activities using socially motivated datasets that are publicly available.

Please note that I am NOT formally trained in the social sciences. For many of these datasets, I have asked colleagues who are experts in the respective fields to collaborate and critique my work.

Finally, I am only ONE person. I am most certainly bringing bias into this work. I have tried to counter my bias by collaborating with others, incorporating others’ experiences, and researching various opinions. I encourage you to question my statements and perform your own research on each dataset.
